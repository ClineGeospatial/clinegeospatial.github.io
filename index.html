Transportation Safety Is Spatial.

Project Highlight: Crash Geocoding for NC Safety Analysis

In support of North Carolina’s traffic safety initiatives, Cline Geospatial provided manual crash geocoding services to improve the positional accuracy of crash records. By aligning crash reports with real-world road network data and field-verified mile markers, our work enabled precise identification of high-crash corridors. These insights directly supported NCDOT and Vision Zero planning teams in prioritizing locations for safety improvements such as signal modifications, lane redesigns, and pedestrian enhancements.

This project illustrates the impact of detailed GIS QA/QC workflows and manual review in transportation planning. Through precise spatial correction, we improved the analytical foundation for corridor safety scoring, crash clustering, and project funding justification.

About the Owner

Angela Cline is a North Carolina native, a graduate of the United States Naval Academy, and a proud veteran with prior federal service as a geospatial analyst. Her experience in the federal government reinforced a core belief: excellent performance must be defined by impact and grounded in integrity.

That principle inspired her to launch Cline Geospatial—an independent, woman-owned GIS consulting firm focused on technical excellence and real-world results. Angela built her company to challenge the status quo: she saw too many agencies weighed down by bloated contracts and inflexible systems. Cline Geospatial was born to be different.

Today, Angela leads a small, skilled team of GIS professionals who value clarity, efficiency, and craftsmanship. From transportation analytics to enterprise GIS integration, Cline Geospatial delivers results that empower clients, not dependency.
